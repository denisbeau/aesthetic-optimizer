# Gemini Deep Research Prompts: Looksmaxxing App Development

**Purpose:** Two chained research prompts for developing an addictive and profitable looksmaxxing app, compliant with Canadian law.

**Strategy:** Chain two focused prompts - first identifies addictive features, second designs monetization leveraging those features. This maintains understanding between both aspects while achieving better results than a single complex prompt.

---

## PROMPT 1: ADDICTIVE FEATURES RESEARCH

**Context (Input):**
Research request for developing a looksmaxxing mobile app targeting users seeking to improve their physical appearance. The app must maximize user engagement and retention through psychologically-driven addictive design patterns. This research will inform the second prompt on monetization strategies.

**Task (Function):**
Conduct comprehensive research on evidence-based psychological mechanisms and design patterns that create addictive user engagement in mobile apps, specifically applied to looksmaxxing/self-improvement contexts. Identify features, mechanics, and behavioral triggers that maximize daily active users, session frequency, and long-term retention.

**Constraints (Parameters):**
- **Legal compliance:** Only include features and mechanisms that are 100% legal in Canada. Exclude anything illegal.
- **Risk avoidance:** Exclude grey-area practices that could pose legal or regulatory risk in Canada, even if technically legal.
- **Scope:** Focus on engagement and retention mechanisms, not monetization (that's Prompt 2).
- **Evidence-based:** Prioritize mechanisms supported by peer-reviewed research or established behavioral psychology principles.
- **Practical:** Focus on implementable features, not theoretical concepts.

**Format (Output):**
Structure the research as follows:

### 1. CORE ADDICTIVE MECHANISMS
List 8-12 primary psychological mechanisms that drive addictive engagement, with:
- Brief explanation of the mechanism
- How it applies to looksmaxxing/self-improvement context
- Evidence base (research support level: strong/moderate/weak)
- Specific app features that implement this mechanism

### 2. FEATURE RECOMMENDATIONS
For each mechanism, provide:
- Feature name and description
- Implementation details (how it works in-app)
- Expected engagement metrics (session frequency, retention impact)
- Legal status in Canada (explicitly state: legal/illegal/grey - exclude grey)

### 3. BEHAVIORAL TRIGGERS
Identify:
- Push notification strategies that drive re-engagement
- In-app triggers that extend session time
- Social/community features that create habit loops
- Progress tracking systems that maintain motivation

### 4. RETENTION SYSTEMS
Describe:
- Streak systems and habit formation mechanics
- Progress visualization that creates investment
- Social comparison features (if legal and ethical)
- Gamification elements that maintain long-term engagement

### 5. RISK ASSESSMENT
For each recommended feature:
- Canadian legal status (legal/illegal/grey)
- Regulatory risk level (low/medium/high)
- If grey area or high risk: exclude from recommendations

**Output Style:**
- Concise, actionable recommendations
- Bullet points and short paragraphs
- Focus on implementable features
- Exclude theoretical discussions

## PROMPT 2: MONETIZATION STRATEGY RESEARCH

**Context (Input):**
You are designing monetization strategies for a looksmaxxing mobile app. The app has already been designed with psychologically-driven addictive features and engagement mechanisms based on comprehensive research. The complete research document from Prompt 1 is provided below:

[PASTE OUTPUT FROM PROMPT 1 HERE]

**Critical Context for Monetization Design:**

The app implements the following core addictive mechanisms that drive high engagement:
- **Variable Ratio Reinforcement:** Unpredictable AI attractiveness ratings create dopamine-driven reward anticipation
- **Zeigarnik Effect:** Incomplete roadmaps and daily tasks create cognitive tension requiring app return
- **Loss Aversion:** Streak systems with "Freeze" mechanics leverage fear of losing progress (users with 7+ day streaks are 2.4x more likely to return)
- **Social Validation:** Accountability circles and peer validation create tribal reward loops
- **Endowment Effect:** Digital Vault of progress photos and personalized data creates investment attachment
- **Flow State:** Structured routines with immediate feedback induce time-loss immersion
- **Commitment Bias:** Public pledges and archetype selection create psychological barriers to quitting
- **Identity-Based Habits:** Users adopt new identities (e.g., "high-status man") requiring daily maintenance
- **Reciprocity Loops:** Free initial facial analysis creates psychological debt for further engagement

**Key Features Requiring Monetization Integration:**
1. **AI Facial Landmark Reveal & Rating Engine** - Variable ratings (1-10) + 100+ landmark scans. High session frequency, strong Day 1 retention.
2. **Protagonist's Roadmap** - 90-day transformation with daily micro-tasks. Improved 30-day retention, increased session length.
3. **Jawline Consistency Streaks** - Multi-day tracking with "Freeze" mechanics. Significant DAU/MAU boost, habitual return visits.
4. **Aesthetic Potential AI Visualizer** - Generative AI showing optimized future self. High investment score, drives detailed data input.
5. **Archetype-Based Accountability Circles** - 5-10 person peer groups. Improved Day 7-90 retention through social accountability.

**Legal Framework:** All features are LEGAL in Canada with PIPEDA/Law 25 compliance. Requirements include: express biometric consent, local-first storage, Privacy by Design defaults, no dark patterns, Competition Act disclaimers, granular sharing controls, Right to Erasure support.

**Engagement Metrics to Preserve:** DAU/MAU ratio exceeding 30%, Day 1 retention, 30-day retention, session frequency, streak maintenance rates.

**Task (Function):**
Research and design evidence-based monetization strategies for looksmaxxing/self-improvement mobile apps. Create revenue models that seamlessly integrate with the addictive features identified above, maximizing Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) while maintaining or enhancing the engagement metrics. Each monetization method must leverage the psychological mechanisms already in place rather than disrupting them.

**Constraints (Parameters):**
- **Legal Compliance:** Only include monetization methods that are 100% legal in Canada. Exclude anything illegal or in grey areas.
- **Risk Avoidance:** Exclude grey-area practices that could pose legal or regulatory risk, even if technically legal.
- **Feature Integration:** Monetization must enhance or complement the addictive features from Prompt 1, not conflict with them. For example, premium features should deepen variable ratio reinforcement, not remove it.
- **Engagement Preservation:** Revenue models must maintain or improve existing engagement metrics (DAU/MAU, retention, session frequency, streak rates).
- **Profitability Focus:** Prioritize revenue models with highest earning potential: subscriptions, in-app purchases, premium feature tiers.
- **Evidence-Based:** Prioritize monetization strategies with proven success in similar app categories (fitness, self-improvement, habit-tracking apps).
- **Canadian Market:** All pricing must be in CAD with consideration for Canadian purchasing power and app store dynamics.

**Format (Output):**
Structure the research as follows:

### 1. REVENUE MODEL ANALYSIS
Compare 5-8 monetization models with detailed analysis:
- **Revenue Potential:** Estimated ARPU (Average Revenue Per User) per month
- **Implementation Complexity:** Development effort required (low/medium/high)
- **Integration with Addictive Features:** How each model leverages or enhances the mechanisms from Prompt 1 (variable reinforcement, streaks, social validation, etc.)
- **Legal Status in Canada:** Explicitly state: legal/illegal (exclude grey areas)
- **Industry Benchmarks:** Revenue data from similar apps in fitness/self-improvement categories
- **User Experience Impact:** How each model affects engagement metrics

### 2. PREMIUM FEATURE RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the addictive features from Prompt 1, identify:
- **Free vs Premium Feature Allocation:** Which features should be free (to maintain engagement hooks) vs premium (to drive conversion)
- **Premium Tier Structure:** Multiple tiers that maximize conversion (e.g., Basic/Premium/Elite)
- **Feature Gating Strategies:** How to gate features to drive upgrades without breaking engagement loops (e.g., limit AI scans per day on free tier, unlimited on premium)
- **Free-to-Paid Conversion Funnels:** Specific user journeys that convert free users to paying customers at optimal moments (e.g., after first streak milestone, when hitting roadmap checkpoint)

### 3. SUBSCRIPTION STRATEGY
Design a subscription model with:
- **Tier Structure:** Free/Basic/Premium tiers with clear differentiation
- **Pricing Recommendations:** Specific CAD pricing for each tier with justification
- **Value Proposition:** Clear value prop for each tier that enhances addictive features (e.g., "Unlock unlimited AI scans to maximize your glow-up potential")
- **Addictive Feature Enhancement:** How subscriptions make the core mechanisms more powerful (e.g., premium users get more streak freezes, advanced roadmap features, exclusive circles)
- **Retention Mechanics:** How subscription features reduce churn (e.g., annual plans with streak protection)

### 4. IN-APP PURCHASE OPPORTUNITIES
Identify specific purchase opportunities:
- **One-Time Purchases:** Non-consumable items that complement addictive features (e.g., "Unlock Advanced Facial Analysis Pack")
- **Consumable Purchases:** Items that create recurring revenue (e.g., "Streak Freeze Tokens," "Extra AI Scans")
- **Non-Consumable Purchases:** High perceived value items (e.g., "Lifetime Premium Features," "Exclusive Archetype Unlocks")
- **Pricing Strategies:** Specific CAD pricing with psychological pricing principles (e.g., $4.99 vs $5.00)
- **Purchase Triggers:** When to surface IAPs without breaking engagement (e.g., after completing first roadmap milestone)

### 5. ADVERTISING INTEGRATION (IF APPLICABLE)
If ads are legal and appropriate for this app category:
- **Ad Placement Strategy:** Where ads can be placed without disrupting engagement loops or breaking flow state
- **Ad Formats:** Compatible formats (e.g., rewarded video, native ads) that enhance rather than detract from addictive features
- **Revenue Potential:** Estimated ARPU from advertising
- **User Experience Trade-offs:** How ads affect retention and engagement metrics
- **Hybrid Approach:** How ads can work alongside subscriptions (e.g., remove ads with premium subscription)

### 6. HYBRID MONETIZATION MODEL
Recommend the optimal combination:
- **Revenue Stream Combination:** How subscriptions, IAPs, and ads (if applicable) work together
- **Synergy Analysis:** How different monetization methods reinforce each other (e.g., IAPs drive subscription upgrades)
- **Revenue Projections:** Estimated monthly ARPU for the hybrid model with conversion rate assumptions
- **Implementation Priority:** Order of implementation (e.g., subscriptions first, then IAPs, then ads)
- **User Segmentation:** Different monetization strategies for different user types (e.g., power users vs casual users)

### 7. RISK ASSESSMENT
For each recommended monetization method:
- **Canadian Legal Status:** Explicitly state legal/illegal (exclude grey areas)
- **Regulatory Risk Level:** Low/medium/high risk assessment
- **Consumer Protection Compliance:** Requirements under Competition Act, PIPEDA, provincial laws
- **Exclusion Criteria:** If any method is grey area or high risk, exclude it from recommendations

**Output Style:**
- Concise, actionable recommendations with specific implementation guidance
- All pricing in CAD with justification
- Revenue projections with assumptions clearly stated
- Focus on implementable strategies, exclude theoretical discussions
- Reference how each monetization method specifically leverages the addictive mechanisms from Prompt 1
